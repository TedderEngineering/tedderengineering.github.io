### Safety Car / Full Course Yellow Procedures (Article 41)

All series substantially identical:

1. **Declaration**: Race Director declares SC/FCY for safety reasons
2. **Signals**: Double yellow flags at all marshal posts; "SC" on DID and timing monitors; announcement on Race Control Radio Frequency
3. **Effective from FIRST of**: radio announcement, timing monitor, DID, double yellow flags, or yellow lights
4. **Car behavior**: Slow to safe speed; overtaking PROHIBITED; single file behind leader/SC
5. **Pit Entry**: CLOSED at first indication of SC/FCY
6. **SC deployment**: SC picks up overall leader; wave-by of cars between SC and leader (pass on right)
7. **Pass-around**: Cars ahead of their class leader may be directed to pass around before pit opens
8. **Ineligible SC pass penalty**: Stop and Hold for time equal to 2 race laps
9. **Closed pit entry rules**:
   - Drive through without stopping = no penalty
   - Emergency Stop permitted for: out of fuel, tire puncture, mechanical damage, driver over max time
   - Emergency Stop requires full-service stop within 3 laps of pit entry being declared open
10. **Restart**: SC extinguishes lights on final SC/FCY lap; yellow flags withdrawn; green flag to resume
11. Each SC lap counts as a race lap

### Fuel Procedures

**All series**: VP Fuels is Official Fuel Supplier; all fuel must be purchased on site. Fuel storage prohibited under awnings or in garages.

**GTWCA — Pit lane refueling PERMITTED** (Art. 37.8):
- Via fuel rig per FIA Drawing 252-7 (single autonomous supply tank per car)
- Max 1 fuel rig per car; rigs may not move during sessions
- Car must be electrically grounded before refueling (independent copper wire)
- Engine off during refueling; driver may stay in car
- Mandatory fire attendant (10lb extinguisher, must stand in position throughout) + dead man valve operator
- Fuel transfer to rig prohibited while car being refueled
- No crossing under/over fuel or vent hose during pit stop
- Fuel rigs may only be filled when no other series occupying pit lane
- Refueling permitted from start of Practice 1; NOT permitted during qualifying
- Overflow hose contents must go into container (min 1.5 US gal with fuel coupling)

**GT America / GT4 America / TC America / McLaren Trophy / GR Cup — Pit lane refueling PROHIBITED**:
- Refueling only in team's allocated paddock area under full safety procedures

**Supplemental Fuel Regs** (applies to GTWCA pit lane refueling):
- Fuel rig restrictor: default 33mm diameter, min 1mm length (FIA Drawing 257A-2)
- Self-closing deadman valve required (max 38mm / 1.5 inch ID, 90-degree)
- Electrical grounding to earth via pit wall ground wire
- Tank top surface max 2m above track; base min 2 m² with self-braking castors

### Penalty and Incident Procedures (Article 49)

**Race Director may assess without Stewards** (Art. 49.5):
- Track limit violations
- Pit lane speed violations
- Blocking
- Unfair advantage gained

Race Director penalty types: Drive Through, Stop & Go, Stop + time, deleted/invalidated laps, time penalties.

**Stewards may impose** (Art. 49.8):
- a) Time penalty (served at next pit stop, or added to elapsed time post-race)
- b) Drive-through penalty
- c) Stop & Go / Stop + time (no work permitted on car)
- d) Change of finishing position
- e) Grid position drop for next race
- f) Pit lane start
- g) Probation
- h) Exclusion from race, next race, or event
- i) Formal reprimand
- j) Monetary fine
- k) Championship points reduction

**Warning Points** (Art. 50):
- 0–3 WP: incident where all cars continue
- 3–5 WP: incident where car(s) cannot continue
- 5 WP accumulated: minimum pit lane start at next race
- 8 WP accumulated: automatic SRO Sporting Board review, possible suspension

**Procedural Penalties** (Art. 49.21):
- Fines, loss of track time for non-sporting infractions (late to briefing, unauthorized vehicles, etc.)
- Cannot be protested or appealed

### Parc Ferme / Impound (Article 44)

- Only supervision Officials may enter Parc Ferme
- Tire supplier representatives (Pirelli/Continental) may check pressures
- Crew members forbidden from remaining once car has stopped
- Entrant representative must remain available to Scrutineering Officials
- Area between Control Line and Parc Ferme entrance is also Parc Ferme
- Duration: Maximum 1 hour from checkered flag (unless car selected for further examination)
- During red flag: Cars under Parc Ferme conditions unless Race Control states otherwise

### Red Flag / Race Suspension (Article 42)

- Race Director orders red flags at all marshal posts and Control Line
- Official Session Clock does NOT stop during red flag
- Suspension time may be added to remaining race time (with Steward approval and timetable constraints)
- **Weather-related red flag**: Cars directed to pit boxes; Parc Ferme conditions; no crew may approach
- **Non-weather red flag**: Cars stop behind SC in single file; Parc Ferme conditions; no crew may approach
- **Restart countdown**: 10 min → 5 min → 3 min → 1 min → 15 sec → Drivers Start Your Engines (DSYE)
- Tire change (slick to wet) may be authorized by Race Director between 10-min and 5-min boards
- **Permitted work from 10-min board**: Assist driver, check tire pressures/temps, check brakes, connect external jumper battery, change tires (if authorized)
- Unauthorized work penalty: Stop + time (TBD by Stewards)
- **If race cannot resume**: Classification based on last scored lap preceding red flag

### Pit Stop Operations (Article 32)

All series:
- Maximum 4 working mechanics in working lane during pit stop
- Engine must be switched off during any pit stop
- Car may only leave working area with no external assistance (unless Race Director permission)
- Cars may move from fast lane into working lane max 3 pit boxes before their own
- Lollipop sign permitted (stop and go indication only)
- Carry-on/backpack compressed gas bottles for tire changes: prohibited
- After pit stop, car may only move after driver safety harness and racing net fastened
- Cars in fast lane at pit speed limit have priority over those leaving working lane
